Neptune

The eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in our Solar System. It is a gas giant with a deep blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.

Roman Mythology

Neptune is named after the Roman god of the sea. In Roman mythology, Neptune was the god of the sea and rivers, and was often depicted with a trident.

Neptune's Discovery

Neptune was discovered in 1846 by the German astronomer Johann Gottfried Galle, based on mathematical predictions made by French mathematician Urbain Le Verrier.
